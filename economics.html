Economic Factors and Statistics

Cost structure

The cost structure of the reforming process shows that 80–85% of the costs are for raw materials, 8–11% for energy, and about 8% for catalysts. This makes the process capital-intensive but economically feasible.

(Orlov, 2021)

Profitability and petrochemicals

Catalytic reforming provides the production of high-octane gasoline, aromatic compounds and hydrogen, which increases the profits of refineries and enhances the role of petrochemicals in Economics.

(Lee & Zhang, 2024)
