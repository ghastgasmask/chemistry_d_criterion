Products & Applications

High-Value Outputs from Naphtha Reforming

Reformate

High-Octane Gasoline Component

The primary product of naphtha reforming is reformate, a high-octane gasoline component that is essential for modern fuel. Through the catalytic reforming process, low-octane naphtha is upgraded to meet the requirements of today's engines.

What is Octane Number?

Octane number is a measure of a fuel's resistance to knocking or pre-ignition during combustion in an engine. Higher octane fuels allow for increased power & fuel efficiency.

Hydrogen

Production

The naphtha reforming process generates a substantial amount of hydrogen as a byproduct.

For example, converting cyclohexane to benzene releases three molecules of hydrogen for every molecule of benzene produced.

Applications

The hydrogen produced is essential for:

  • Hydrotreating: Removing sulfur, nitrogen, and other impurities from petroleum fractions
  • Hydrocracking: This reaction involves the breaking of larger hydrocarbon molecules into smaller ones in the presence of hydrogen. While it can produce lighter, more volatile components, excessive hydrocracking is generally undesirable as it can lead to a loss of valuable liquid products and consume hydrogen
